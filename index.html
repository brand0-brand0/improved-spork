Changing your GitHub repository from private to public is a straightforward process. Here’s how you can do it:

  1. Navigate to Your Repository: Log into your GitHub account, go to your dashboard, and click on the repository you want to change to public.
  2. Go to Settings: Once you’re in your repository, look for the "Settings" tab near the top-right corner of the page and click on it.
  3. Find the "Danger Zone": Scroll all the way down to the bottom of the settings page until you find a section labeled "Danger Zone".
  4. Change Repository Visibility: In the Danger Zone, you’ll see a button or link that says "Change repository visibility". Click on it.
  5. Choose Public: You will be prompted to confirm your action. Select “Public” to make your repository visible to anyone.
  6. Confirm the Change: GitHub will ask you to type your repository name to confirm that you want to change the visibility to public. This is a safety measure to ensure you understand the action you’re taking.
  7. Complete the Change: After typing your repository name, click on the button to change the visibility to public. This might be labeled "I understand, change repository visibility", or something similar.
